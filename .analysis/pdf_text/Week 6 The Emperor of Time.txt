Purpose: 
To help you steward time, define finish lines, and execute what God has entrusted to you. 
 
USING THIS WORKSHEET 
1. Read Slowly 
Begin with Scripture and the opening declaration to reset your perspective. 
 
2. Name Your Season 
Identify what must be finished—and what must be released. 
 
3. Choose Priority Assignments 
One assignment per life domain. Keep it simple. 
 
4. Set Deadlines 
Use the Genesis Deadlines Map to create urgency with clarity. 
 
5. Protect the Finish 
Decide what must stop so completion can happen.

Sign the declaration. Ownership creates momentum.

The Emperor of Time

Perspective Before Execution 
• “Teach us to number our days, that we may gain a heart of wisdom.” Psalm 90:12 
• Time is not random. It is entrusted. God created time, placed you inside it, and assigned 
work for you to finish within it. 
• You cannot control how long you live. But you can control how intentionally you live. 
• This worksheet is not about productivity. It is about stewardship. 
• You are not managing a calendar. You are stewarding a calling. 
 
The Emperor of Time Declaration: My life is disciplined and focused. God has entrusted 
me with time. I will determine my days through prayer, clarity, and obedience. I will not 
surrender my time to distraction, pressure, or fear. I will protect what matters, release what 
doesn’t, and finish what God has given me to do. Time works for me because it belongs to Him. 
Reflection — Season Awareness (Answer honestly. No overthinking.) 
What season of life am I in right now? 
(Discovery / Building / Leading / Finishing / Transition) 
 
What must be finished in this season? 
 
What must be released in this season? 
 
What deserves my best energy right now?

The Commission 
Genesis shows us the pattern:

• God filled. 
• God finished.
